Vidwat Certified · Bharatanatyam

A performer of rare grace and precision

Vidhushi Sindhu's performances weave together Nritta (pure dance), Nritya (expressive dance) and Natya (dramatic presentation) into a seamless and deeply moving experience. Trained under Guru Parvati Vidhyanathan in the Pandanallur bani, her artistry reflects the classical rigor and spiritual depth of South Indian classical tradition.

The Graduation Debut
Arangetram

The Arangetram — a student's first solo public performance — is one of the most sacred milestones in Bharatanatyam. Years of dedicated practice culminate in a full evening of dance, a rite of passage witnessed by the entire community.

Arangetram · The Debut

A moment that
lasts a lifetime

The word Arangetram means "ascending the stage" in Tamil. After years of rigorous training — mastering adavus, mudras, abhinaya and full Margam compositions — the student presents a solo performance of 2–3 hours, demonstrating their complete mastery. It is a celebration for the entire Nrithyalaya family.
